Overview

Nordic log frame

This frame draws inspiration from the Nordic log homes. It can hold pictures of dimensions 6” x 4”. It is self standing but it can be modified to be used as a hanging frame as well.

Process

Step 1

All the measurements in the steps and the drawing images are given in inches.

Cut out four blocks of solid wood according to the given dimensions. Notice that two blocks have a length of 6 3/4” and two have a length of 8 3/4”.

You can use a band-saw or a table saw for doing so. Hand-held tools may be used as well.

Step 2

Give a radius to the lengths of the top faces (edges of length 6 3/4” and 8 3/4”). Make sure the face is completely rounded.

This can be done by either sanding it using sandpaper, sanding machine or a table router of appropriate radius.

Step 3

To join the pieces together, we will be using a cross-lap joint. For this, create notches of width 3/4” on the wooden blocks according to the mentioned dimensions. Note that the notches are created on the curved face for the pieces of length 8 3/4” and the notches for the 6 3/4” length pieces are on the bottom face (face opposite to the curved face).

Accuracy is extremely important in this step to ensure a tight fit when the pieces are joined together. You may use a chisel to manually carve out the notch or use a sawing machines such as table saw or band saw.

Step 4

Use a chisel to cut out the portion (shaded region) that will hold the photograph. Make sure that the cuts are sharp so that the photo sits flat in it.

Use a bench-vice to hold the piece in place. You can also use additional wooden pieces in between the frame piece and the vice to avoid the vice from directly gripping the frame and leaving marks.

Step 5

You can glaze/clearcoat the pieces before or after this step.

Put all the pieces together as shown. If the pieces were cut with accurate measurements, you may be able to fit them tightly without the the need for any adhesive. However, you may use strong wood adhesive for sticking the pieces together. If there are gaps left, you can use wood filler (glue + sawdust) to fill the gap.

Step 6

Add photo-frame points (flexipoint tabs) to the frame. You may use alternatives such as turn-buttons.

Cut a rectangular MDF piece of given dimensions to use as the back cover of the frame. You can use any other suitable material as well.

Step 7

Now you can add a photo and the back cover and close the frame-points to secure the photograph.

You can make modifications to the frame based on how you want to make it stand or to hang on a wall.
